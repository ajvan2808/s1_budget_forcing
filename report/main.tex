\begin{abstract}
Test-time scaling improves language-model reasoning by allocating additional computation during inference rather than only during training. This report reviews \citet{muennighoff2025s1}, which combines a compact reasoning dataset with budget forcing, and then studies whether the budget-forcing component transfers to Vietnamese-language reasoning. We adapt the decoding trigger to Vietnamese, sweep \texttt{n\_wait} $\in \{0,1,2\}$, and evaluate reasoning-distilled, Vietnamese-specialized, and Southeast Asian open models on Vietnamese math, factual multiple-choice, and exploratory high-school exam benchmarks. The study separates mechanical control over thinking length from useful accuracy gains, asking whether extra generated reasoning actually improves answers under realistic compute constraints.
\end{abstract}
